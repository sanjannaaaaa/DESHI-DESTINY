CALCUTTA

Victoria Memorial: A majestic marble structure built in memory of Queen Victoria, offering a glimpse into India's colonial past. Howrah Bridge: An iconic cantilever bridge, connecting Kolkata with Howrah over the Hooghly River, offering breathtaking views especially at dusk. Indian Museum: Established in 1814, it is one of the oldest museums in Asia, housing an extensive collection of artifacts spanning India's history. Dakshineswar Kali Temple: Situated on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River, this temple dedicated to Goddess Kali is renowned for its architectural beauty and religious significance. Kumartuli: Explore the narrow lanes of Kumartuli, where skilled artisans craft intricate clay idols of deities, particularly during the festive season of Durga Puja. Rabindra Sadan: A cultural hub named after the Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore, hosting performances of music, dance, and theater, showcasing Kolkata's artistic vibrancy.
