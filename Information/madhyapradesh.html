MADHYA PRADESH

Khajuraho: Famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Site temples, known for their intricate carvings depicting various aspects of life. Gwalior: Home to the majestic Gwalior Fort, known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and the enchanting Teli Ka Mandir and Sas Bahu temples. Bhopal: The capital city known for its blend of history, culture, and modernity, with attractions like the UNESCO-listed Bhimbetka rock shelters, Taj-ul-Masjid, and the serene Upper Lake. Sanchi: Renowned for its ancient Buddhist stupas, including the Great Stupa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the nearby Sanchi Museum. Orchha: A historical town known for its palaces, temples, and cenotaphs, with highlights such as the Jahangir Mahal, Raj Mahal, and Chaturbhuj Temple. Kanha National Park: One of India's largest and most famous tiger reserves, offering wildlife safaris to spot tigers, leopards, and other animals in their natural habitat. Bandhavgarh National Park: Another renowned tiger reserve known for its high tiger population, along with diverse flora and fauna. Pachmarhi: A hill station known for its scenic beauty, waterfalls, and caves, including the Pandava Caves and Apsara Vihar.
